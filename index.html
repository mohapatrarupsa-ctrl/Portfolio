What is SAP S/4HANA?

SAP S/4HANA (Systems, Applications & Products — 4th generation High-performance ANalytic Appliance) is SAP's modern ERP suite built on the in-memory HANA database. It consolidates previously siloed business processes into one integrated system.

⚙️

Real-time data processing · Simplified data model · Embedded AI & analytics · Cloud-first architecture · Role-based Fiori UX

💡 Your role connects to Finance (FI) and Procurement (MM). These modules share data seamlessly — a purchase order in MM automatically triggers a financial commitment in FI.

🏆 Module 4 · Building Long-term Trust

From Engagement to
Enduring Partnership

Client relationships that last beyond a single project are built on consistent reliability and genuine care. Here are the habits that distinguish trusted advisors in our organisation.

📅

Regular check-ins

Schedule brief weekly syncs even when there's "nothing to report." Consistency builds trust more than big moments do.

🎁

Value between meetings

Share a relevant article, insight, or tool the client wasn't expecting. Generosity without agenda is remembered.

🔄

Close the loop

Always follow up on commitments — even to say it's still in progress. Never let a client wonder about the status.

🧠

Remember what matters

Note client priorities, pressures and personal details. Recalling them later shows you listened and cared.

🚀 Your Next Step

Before your next client interaction this week, write down one question you'll ask about their goals — not yours. Building this habit is the single most impactful thing a new leader can do in their first 90 days.

Maya's client — a pharmaceutical company — operates across India, Germany, and the United States. They have inter-company transactions worth €45 million. Her manager has just handed her the lead. The filing deadline is in 6 weeks.

⚖️ Decision Point 1 · Transfer Pricing Method

Which Method Should Maya Apply?

The client's pharmaceutical IP is being licensed from the US entity to Germany and India. Maya must determine the correct transfer pricing method for this transaction under OECD guidelines.

📊 The Facts

Transaction: IP licence (pharmaceutical patent portfolio)
Flow: US → Germany (€28M) · US → India (€17M)
Client's prior approach: Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP) method
Available benchmarking data: Limited external comparables for this IP category

What should Maya recommend to her manager?

Continue with the CUP method — stick with what the client knows
Switch to the Profit Split Method — better suited to unique IP
Use TNMM — it's simpler to document
📂
Maya Sharma
Week 3 — Documentation
"The German tax office has flagged our master file. They're asking for additional economic analysis. If we don't respond correctly, we're looking at adjustments — and penalties."
🚨 Compliance Risk
📁 Decision Point 2 · BEPS Documentation

The German Tax Authority Has Questions

Germany's Betriebsprüfung has issued a formal request under §90 AO (German Fiscal Code). They want supplementary economic analysis supporting the Profit Split allocation — and they want it in 4 weeks.

⚠️ What's at Stake

If the documentation is insufficient, the German authority can make a transfer pricing adjustment — potentially reversing the profit allocation and triggering double taxation. Penalties under §162 AO can reach 10% of the adjusted income.

What should Maya's response strategy be?

Submit the original documentation again — it should be sufficient
Request an Advance Pricing Agreement (APA) immediately
Prepare supplementary benchmarking + FAR analysis narrative
🌟
Maya Sharma
6 weeks later — outcome
"We got it right. The German authority accepted our supplementary analysis. No adjustment, no penalty. The client asked me to lead the next phase personally."
✅ Mission Accomplished
🏆 Learning Summary

What Maya — and You — Have Learned

By making the right decisions at each stage, Maya protected her client from a €4.5M potential adjustment and built the trust that comes from deep technical expertise applied with sound professional judgement.

📚 Key Takeaways from this Module

1. Method matters: Match the TP method to the economic reality of the transaction — not the client's preference or convenience.

2. Documentation is defence: Master File + Local File + CbCR is the BEPS minimum standard. Go beyond it for high-value IP transactions.

3. FAR analysis is your foundation: Functions, Assets, Risks — document the economic substance, not just the legal form.
